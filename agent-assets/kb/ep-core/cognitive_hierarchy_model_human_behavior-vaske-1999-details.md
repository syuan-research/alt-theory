===SECTION===
## Study: Colorado Wildland Preservation Survey
[Context: Public Land Management]
The study surveyed a random sample of Colorado residents (n=960) to test the cognitive hierarchy model regarding National Forest management.
---
**Methodology**:
*   **Sample**: Residents from the urban Front Range and rural/mountain regions.
*   **Value Measurement**: 9 basic belief statements were used.
    *   *Anthropocentric examples*: "The primary value of forests is to provide timber, grazing land, and minerals..." or "Forests are valuable only if they produce jobs..."
    *   *Biocentric examples*: "Forests have as much right to exist as people" or "Nature has as much right to exist as people."
*   **Attitude Measurement**: Respondents rated feelings about restoring forests to a natural state and expanding wilderness areas.
*   **Behavioral Intention**: Likelihood of voting for these preservation measures.

===SECTION===
## Empirical Finding: The Value Orientation Continuum
**Results**: Exploratory factor analysis confirmed that the basic belief statements loaded onto two factors: anthropocentric and biocentric.
---
However, confirmatory factor analysis and reliability scores (Cronbach's alpha > .85) supported combining these two factors into a single **Biocentric/Anthropocentric Value Orientation Continuum**. This supports the hypothesis that these are opposite ends of a single spectrum rather than orthogonal (independent) dimensions.

===SECTION===
## Empirical Finding: Full Mediation of Voting Intentions
**Results**: Structural equation modeling (LISREL) was used to test three models: Direct Effects, Partial Mediation, and Full Mediation.
---
*   **Value Orientation $\rightarrow$ Attitude**: Significant positive effect ($\beta = .73$). People with biocentric values held stronger pro-preservation attitudes.
*   **Attitude $\rightarrow$ Voting Intention**: Significant positive effect ($\beta = .94$).
*   **Value Orientation $\rightarrow$ Voting Intention**: In the partial mediation model, the direct path was *not* significant ($\beta = .04$).
*   **Conclusion**: The attitude variable fully mediated the relationship. Values influenced voting *only* through their effect on attitude. The model explained 88% of the variance in voting intentions ($R^2 = .88$).

===SECTION===
## Raw Text Snippet: Anthropocentric Definition
"An anthropocentric value orientation represents a human-centered view of the nonhuman world... The approach assumes that providing for human uses and benefits is the primary aim of natural resource allocation and management... The environment is seen as 'material to be used by humans as they see fit'." (p. 525)

===SECTION===
## Raw Text Snippet: Biocentric Definition
"In contrast, a biocentric value orientation is a nature-centered or ecocentered approach. The value of all ecosystems, species, and natural organisms is elevated to center stage... This approach assumes that environmental objects have inherent as well as instrumental worth... these inherent values are to be equally respected and preserved, even if they conflict with human centered-values." (p. 526)
