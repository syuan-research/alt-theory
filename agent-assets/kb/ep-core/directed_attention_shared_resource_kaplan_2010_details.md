## Empirical Study: The Chicago Public Housing Research
[Context: Urban Environment]
A study by Kuo and Sullivan (2001a) investigated residents of a Chicago public housing facility where some buildings had trees/grass nearby while others were surrounded by concrete.
---
**Results**: Residents with nature near their apartments were significantly less likely to use aggression or violence in dealing with problems. 
**Statistical Analysis**: The effect on reduced aggression was found to be mediated by differences in attention and memory capacity (measured by Digit Span Backwards). This provides direct evidence that natural environments support self-regulation by maintaining the attentional resource.

## Evidence: The Failure of Television as Restoration
Television is analyzed as a "self-distraction device" that provides "hard fascination."
---
**Findings**: Although television offers a quick way to shut down "internal clutter," research by Kubey and Csikszentmihalyi (1990) shows that viewers feel more passive, less alert, and more irritable after watching.
---
**Conclusion**: Unlike natural environments, TV creates "attentional capture" that precludes the reflection needed to resolve persistent problems. It acts as an escapist mechanism that increases mental fatigue over time rather than reducing it.

## Experimental Study: SART and Nature Pictures (Berto, 2005)
This study used the Sustained Attention Response Task (SART), a "go/no-go" task, to measure the impact of restorative environments.
---
**Methodology**: Participants performed a depleting task and then viewed pictures of either restorative environments (lakes, mountains) or nonrestorative environments (skyscrapers, streets).
---
**Results**: Only participants who viewed restorative nature pictures improved their performance on the SART. This confirms that even passive, pictorial exposure to nature can partially restore the executive portions of the attention network.

## Empirical Data: Glucose and Heuristic Decision Making
[Context: Resource Depletion]
Masicampo and Baumeister (2008) explored how the common resource relates to decision-making.
---
**Findings**: Fatigued participants showed a higher reliance on heuristics (shortcuts). Drinking lemonade with real sugar (glucose) eliminated this reliance, while lemonade with artificial sweeteners had no effect.
---
**Theoretical Note**: This supports the idea that the resource is energetically based, though the authors caution that glucose may merely "offset" depletion effects rather than "restore" the mechanism in the way that nature interaction does.

## Raw Text Snippet: James on Volitional Effort
"William James (1892) would hardly have been surprised to hear that self-regulation and executive function tasks seem to draw on the same resource... James proposed that volitional effort was characteristic of both... As he succinctly put it, 'Volitional effort is the effort of attention' (1892, p. 317)."

## Raw Text Snippet: Soft Fascination vs. Internal Noise
"Unresolved problems tend to lead to persisting memories... these unresolved problems could create a kind of internal noise that would lead to excessive demands on directed attention... Natural environments... are able to capture involuntary attention without monopolizing attentional channel capacity." (p. 49)
